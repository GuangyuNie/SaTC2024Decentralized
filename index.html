Background

Recent advances in generative models have enabled the creation of synthetic contents that are indistinguishable even by naked eyes. Such successes raised serious concerns regarding emerging threats due to the applications of generative models. One typical application is malicious personation, where the attacker creates fake content and disseminate inappropriate or illegal contents. To resolve these problems, we propose model attribution and secure training.

Objective

In the first research task, we consider threats related to the dissemination and use of generative models, including fake news generation and IP theft of generative arts. In the second, we consider threats of data leak during collaborative training of generative models. Such collaborations on proprietary data becomes necessary in order to reduce model bias and/or data acquisition cost.

Challenge

1) Challenge 1: Poor tradeoff among robust attribution accuracy, generation quality, and key capacity from existing watermarking methods, even for known attacks and models.
2) Challenge 2: Poor tradeoff between training security and scalability from existing secure training methods: DP training does not achieve data reconstruction hardness; MPC is too costly to scale.
